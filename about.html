We are a team of students working towards equal representation and opportunities for women and other minorities across all professions. We encourage all women to become educated on the status and success of women in their field and provide resources to do so through our website, including information on Title IX and sexual harrassment policies for universities across the United States. Through our outreach efforts, we stimulate new discussions on how we can level the playing field in every discipline.


the team


Arielle Summitt

Arielle is a sophomore neuroscience and pre-med major, with other interests in computer science and music. She first thought of the concept of feministudent when she took a class at Johns Hopkins entitled "Ladies in the Laboratory: Science and Gender in U.S. History." There, her interest in gender inequality in academia and the professions was kindled. As she searched for further reading on the topic, it occurred to her that it would be difficult for many to find these resources, as they were scattered throughout libraries, apps, and the web. She began feministudent as a website where users could find books and podcasts on any topic in academia. With the help of Andrea, it has quickly grown into much more.




Andrea Newman-Rivera

Andrea is also a sophomore studying Public Health and Molecular and Cellular Biology with interests in running and pop-art. Originally from Corozal, Puerto Rico, Andrea spent her elementary and middle school years in Ames, IA. After moving back to Puerto Rico for high school, she acquired a passion for improving educational opportunity for all, especially after seeing drastic differences between school systems across the United States and the world. Through a partnership with Arielle, feministudent aims to close the opportunity gaps women face during their cradle to career pathway by empowerment, laughter, and free educational resources.

Equal representation of women and other minority groups in professional fields, including women in higher paid and managerial positions.


A higher standard of education and greater opportunities for women and other minorities.


An end to hostile attitudes toward women and sexual harrassment in the workplace, as well as greater accountability and appropriate punishment of perpetrators.

We are based at Johns Hopkins University, located in Baltimore MD. We are currently focusing our high school outreach resources in the Baltimore County area, but we hope to expand beyond this boundary in the future. Our work with undergraduates through women in the workforce is nationwide in the U.S. Our website is for everyone everywhere.
